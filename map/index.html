IU Campus Crime

This project maps data provided by the Indiana University Police Department concerning crimes on IU's campus between 2013 - 2015. We hope this project illuminates which areas of campus have the most crimes and allow students to plan for their safety.

By James Benedict, Jason Choi, Jialiang Wang and Zhenkai Liu

Main Findings

  1. Crimes cluster around residential areas and increase during the school year
  2. IUPD responds to crimes off campus as well as on campus
  3. Overwhelmingly the crimes are non-violent
  4. We question if blue safety lights are in the most opportune location
  5. Sexual crimes go underreported to the police


Data descriptions

Due to the lack of consistencey of reported crime names (e.g. "DUI" vs "Driving with a BAC about 0.8") and to simplify the map we grouped certain crimes together.

Traffic: 478, Theft: 365, Possession: 318, Nuisance: 200, Marijuana: 195, Harassment: 74, Assault: 72, DUI: 49, Trespass: 45, Other: 41, Property: 28, Sexual Misconduct: 12, Rape: 10, Drug Sale: 8, Domestic: 4, Stalking: 4, Arson: 2.



Data issues

Data about campus crime was provided by the Indiana University Police Department for the years 2013 - 2015. It represents calls to the police. On our further review we found half of the months were missing from this dataset. Additionally, 1/3 of the months provided had less than 99% percent of the crimes compared to the other months. This leads us to believe there was only 4 reliable months of data provided by the IUPD.

Further we believe there was systematic under reporting of sexual crimes to IUPD. According to IU Clery report there were 39 reported rapes at Indiana University in 2015. Our data shows of these only six were reported to the police. We believe this demonstrates an lack of willingness to report sexual assaults directly to the police.

We also were unable to locate 150 additional crimes due to unidentifiable reported locations.

Analyzed Crimes

TakeawayWhile this reduced clutter, we still need to filter more to find really relevant information. One trend we immediately noticed was IUPD responded to crimes outside of the immediate campus area. This counters the official position of IUPD that they only respond to crimes on campus. This was especially relevant this year when the IUPD claimed the reason they didn't issue a safety warning to students after a masked man broke into multiple house because it did not occur on campus. If they responded to some crimes off campus, why would they ignore this one?
